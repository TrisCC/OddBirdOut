# Odd Bird Out project description

Odd Bird Out is a three-player interactive game installation about cyber ostracism: the experience of being ignored, excluded or rejected within a digital social environment. The work translates this psychological/social phenomenon into a short arcade-style game in which three players compete as ostriches collecting seeds to hatch a Golden Egg. The project builds on the idea that digital exclusion can feel (im)personal: no one may openly reject you, but the game still makes you feel left out.

The installation consists of a triangular central setup with three player stations around it. Each player uses their own tablet, separated by panels/curtains so they cannot see the others’ screens. Spectators can stand around the outside and observe the players as part of the exhibition setup. The top-down sketch shows the three players placed around the central structure within an approximately 2.5 m interaction zone.

In the game, each round offers three actions: Share, Peck, or Head in Sand. Players can give seeds to another ostrich, steal seeds, or protect themselves. The shared objective is to collect enough seeds to hatch the Golden Egg.

The game begins normally, so players trust that they are seeing real actions from the others. After a few rounds, the system starts manipulating what each player sees. Every player is shown a different version of the game in which the other two players appear to cooperate and exclude them. This means all players privately experience being the “odd bird out,” even though everyone is being manipulated at the same time.

Because this happens separately on each tablet, every participant believes that they are the odd one out. Player A sees B and C excluding them, Player B sees A and C excluding them, and Player C sees A and B excluding them. The actual social situation is therefore different from the displayed social situation. The interface produces the feeling of exclusion, even though all players are being subjected to the same manipulation.

The experience ends with a reveal: the players discover that they were not individually rejected, but that the system made each of them feel excluded.

Visually, the work uses an ostrich-themed pixel-art style, with simple characters, seeds, a Golden Egg, action buttons, hearts and broken hearts.
